SMART-JAM is our project idea for traffic jam forecasting in the short term, mainly looking at what could happen within the next 60 minutes. The motivation is pretty basic: congestion builds fast, and once it is clearly visible on the road, you are already late. That is why we focus on prediction rather than only describing current traffic conditions.

In SMART-JAM, we frame the task as learning from time based traffic observations and then producing a simple forecast for the near future, especially the 30 to 60 minute window. We are not trying to “solve traffic” or claim perfect coverage. The point is to explore whether patterns in the available data can support useful short term signals at all.

What we want from SMART-JAM is something understandable: a forecast output that can be interpreted quickly, without requiring deep technical knowledge. In our context, it is more about building a clear pipeline from data to a forecast view, and showing how a model could be used for near term congestion estimation. SMART-JAM also helps us be honest about what short-term traffic forecasting can and cannot do in practice. A lot depends on the inputs. If the data is messy, missing areas, or not updated often enough, the forecast will naturally have gaps and uncertainty. So part of the project is showing how the quality of the data and how frequently it changes shapes what kind of prediction is even reasonable, and where the approach starts to break down.
